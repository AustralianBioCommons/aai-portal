Australian BioCommons Acceptable Use Policy and Conditions of Use

Release date: 5th March 2026

This Acceptable Use Policy and Conditions of Use ("AUP") defines the rules and conditions that govern your access to and use (including transmission, processing, and storage of data) of the resources and services ("Services") as granted by Australian BioCommons for the purpose of discovering, creating, storing, transferring, manipulating, analysing, and interpreting life science data in:

  • research, or
  • research training, or
  • innovation that contributes to Australian economic outcomes, national security, social wellbeing and environmental sustainability.

The Services are available to Australian academic, government, and industry researchers and their collaborators. Some Services are also available to international researchers. See service level agreements listed below for detailed access conditions.

If you are unsure whether your intended use complies with these rules and conditions, or if you seek permission for an intended use that does not fall within these rules and conditions, please reach out to the administrative contact referenced below.

For the purposes of this AUP, "Australian BioCommons" is defined as The University of Melbourne, in its role as lead agent of the Australian BioCommons Project, plus the Service-specific Operational Partners and Computational Partners described in the service level agreements listed below.
